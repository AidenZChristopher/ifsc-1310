Generation to Generation

LEGO was founded by Ole Kirk Christiansen, a Danish carpenter, in 1932 in Billund, Denmark. Originally, Christiansen crafted wooden toys in his small workshop. The name " LEGO" comes from the Danish phrase "leg godt," meaning "play well."

In 1947, the company transitioned from wood to plastic, introducing the iconic interlocking LEGO bricks in 1949, and refining their design into the form we know today by 1958. After Ole Kirk's passing in 1958, leadership was handed down to his son, Godtfred Kirk Christiansen. Under Godtfred, LEGO expanded significantly, introducing themed sets and the innovative LEGO Minifigure in 1978.

In 1979, Godtfred’s son, Kjeld Kirk Kristiansen, took over leadership, becoming CEO and further globalizing the brand. Kjeld introduced innovations like LEGO Technic and drove major international growth until stepping down in 2004. The Christiansen family has maintained ownership and influence, now spanning four generations, making LEGO one of the most successful family-run businesses worldwide.

Facts about the Kristiansens

Ole Kirk Kristiansen

  • Birth and Early Life
  • Ole Kirk Kristiansen was born on April 7, 1891, in Omvraa Mark, Filskov parish, Denmark.
  • He was one of 13 siblings and grew up in a modest but nurturing home.
  • Childhood and Apprenticeship
  • Began working at age seven herding livestock and helping on nearby farms.
  • Later apprenticed as a carpenter and joiner under his brother Kresten in Give.
  • Earned his journeyman’s certificate in 1911 at the age of 20.
  • Military Service and Journeyman Travels:
  • Served as an infantryman at the Citadel in Copenhagen.
  • Traveled and worked across Denmark and Norway, including time in Haderslev, the island of Als, and a wood products factory in Vestfossen.
  • Studied at Haslev Technical School.
  • Was recalled to the army several times during World War I.

Godtfred Kirk Christiansen

  • Birth and Early Life
  • Born on 8 July 1920 in Denmark, third son of Ole Kirk Christiansen.
  • Grew up in a poor family with limited formal education.
  • Helped in his father's woodworking shop from a young age.
  • Grew up in a poor family with limited formal education.
  • Accidentally caused a fire in 1924 that destroyed the shop.
  • Designed wooden toys by age 17 and studied at Haslev Technical College.
  • Lego Brick Invention
  • In 1946, LEGO moved into plastic with injection molding machines.
  • Developed the Lego brick design with interlocking clutch tubes, patented on 28 January 1958.
  • Credited with refining the iconic Lego brick clutch system that ensures sturdiness.
  • Later Years and Succession
  • Married to Edith Kirk Christiansen; had three children: Gunhild, Kjeld, and Hanne.
  • Stepped down as managing director in 1973, became chairman of the board.
  • His son, Kjeld Kirk Kristiansen, took over as CEO in 1979.
  • Retired from his chairman role in 1993.

Kjeld Kirk Kristiansen

  • Birth and Early Life
  • Born 27 December 1947 in Billund, Denmark; grandson of LEGO founder Ole Kirk Christiansen.
  • Participated in early LEGO product testing and marketing as a child.
  • Earned a bachelor’s degree from Aarhus University and an MBA from IMD in Switzerland (1972).
  • Career at LEGO
  • Served as President and CEO of The LEGO Group (1979–2004).
  • Introduced key innovations: themes, minifigures, LEGO.com, LEGO Mindstorms, and licensed products.
  • Stepped down in 2004 to focus on ownership and board roles, including:
    • Vice-chairman of The LEGO Group
    • Chairman of KIRKBI A/S, LEGO Holding A/S, and the LEGO Foundation.
  • Personal Life
  • Married to Edith Kirk Christiansen; had three children: Gunhild, Kjeld, and Hanne.
  • Married to Camilla, with three children: Thomas, Sofie, and Agnete (an Olympic dressage rider).
  • Owns Strathconon Estate and other properties in the Scottish Highlands.

Fourth Generation (Thomas Kirk Kristiansen) – Sustainability and Eco-Friendly Innovation

  • Born 18 February 1979 in Billund, Denmark; great-grandson of LEGO founder Ole Kirk Christiansen.
  • Grew up closely connected to the LEGO brand, experiencing firsthand the family’s role in global toy innovation.
  • Holds a degree in Marketing and Economics, reflecting early interest in business management.
  • Career at LEGO
  • Joined the LEGO Group after gaining external business experience outside the family company.
  • Appointed Chairman of the Board of LEGO A/S in 2020, succeeding his father, Kjeld Kirk Kristiansen.
  • Oversees strategic initiatives focused on sustainability, including the company’s commitment to using sustainable materials in LEGO bricks by 2030.
  • Actively involved in long-term corporate planning, innovation, and brand positioning to ensure LEGO’s continued global relevance.
  • Personal Life
  • Married to Signe Kirk Kristiansen; together they have children, continuing the family’s generational legacy.
  • Engaged in philanthropy and sustainability projects, reflecting personal commitment to environmental and social responsibility.
  • Advocates for eco-friendly practices within the LEGO Group and the broader toy industry.
