The Invention of LEGO Bricks

The LEGO Group was founded in 1932 in Billund, Denmark, by Ole Kirk Christiansen, a skilled carpenter facing tough economic times during the Great Depression. Originally, the company produced wooden toys, stepladders, and ironing boards. Christiansen named the company “LEGO,” derived from the Danish phrase “leg godt,” meaning “play well.” Unbeknownst to him at the time, “LEGO” also translates to “I put together” in Latin—a fitting coincidence for what the company would become.

As plastic manufacturing gained popularity after World War II, LEGO began experimenting with new materials. In 1949, they released the "Automatic Binding Bricks," early versions of the interlocking pieces we recognize today. These bricks featured a unique stud-and-tube design, allowing for stable, stackable connections. This design was patented in 1958 and remains the core of LEGO construction even now. Though initially met with skepticism, the bricks grew in popularity due to their durability, versatility, and imaginative potential.

Over the decades, LEGO transformed from a small toy manufacturer into a global icon. The company expanded into themed sets, robotics, video games, movies, and even theme parks. But its foundation lies in Ole Kirk Christiansen’s commitment to quality and creativity. What started as a small carpentry business in a Danish workshop became a universal symbol of learning through play—one brick at a time.

Snapping into History

Ole Kirk Kristiansen

  • Birth and Early Life
  • Ole Kirk Kristiansen was born on April 7, 1891, in Omvraa Mark, Filskov parish, Denmark.
  • He was one of 13 siblings and grew up in a modest but nurturing home.
  • Childhood and Apprenticeship
  • Began working at age seven herding livestock and helping on nearby farms.
  • Later apprenticed as a carpenter and joiner under his brother Kresten in Give.
  • Earned his journeyman’s certificate in 1911 at the age of 20.
  • Military Service and Journeyman Travels:
  • Served as an infantryman at the Citadel in Copenhagen.
  • Traveled and worked across Denmark and Norway, including time in Haderslev, the island of Als, and a wood products factory in Vestfossen.
  • Studied at Haslev Technical School.
  • Was recalled to the army several times during World War I.

  • Later Years and Succession
  • Married to Edith Kirk Christiansen; had three children: Gunhild, Kjeld, and Hanne.
  • Stepped down as managing director in 1973, became chairman of the board.
  • His son, Kjeld Kirk Kristiansen, took over as CEO in 1979.
  • Retired from his chairman role in 1993.

Kjeld Kirk Kristiansen

  • Birth and Early Life
  • Born 27 December 1947 in Billund, Denmark; grandson of LEGO founder Ole Kirk Christiansen.
  • Participated in early LEGO product testing and marketing as a child.
  • Earned a bachelor’s degree from Aarhus University and an MBA from IMD in Switzerland (1972).
  • Career at LEGO
  • Served as President and CEO of The LEGO Group (1979–2004).
  • Introduced key innovations: themes, minifigures, LEGO.com, LEGO Mindstorms, and licensed products.
  • Stepped down in 2004 to focus on ownership and board roles, including:
    • Vice-chairman of The LEGO Group
    • Chairman of KIRKBI A/S, LEGO Holding A/S, and the LEGO Foundation.
  • Personal Life
  • Married to Edith Kirk Christiansen; had three children: Gunhild, Kjeld, and Hanne.
  • Married to Camilla, with three children: Thomas, Sofie, and Agnete (an Olympic dressage rider).
  • Owns Strathconon Estate and other properties in the Scottish Highlands.
